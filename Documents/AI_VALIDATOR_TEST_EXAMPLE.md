# AI Validator Test Example

## Problem Post

**Title:** My tomato plants have yellow leaves and stunted growth

**Content:** I'm growing tomatoes in my field. The leaves are turning yellow from bottom to top, and the plants are not growing well. The soil is clay-heavy and I water them every day. What should I do?

---

## 15 Solutions (Comments)

### ✅ BEST SOLUTIONS (Score: 70-100)

**Solution 1 (Score: ~85-95):**
This is nitrogen deficiency. Add organic compost or well-rotted cow manure (5kg per plant). Mix neem cake (200g per plant) into soil. Test soil pH - tomatoes need 6.0-6.8. Reduce watering to every 2-3 days as clay soil retains water. Apply foliar spray of 19:19:19 NPK (2g per liter) weekly for 3 weeks. Mulch with dry leaves to prevent nutrient loss.

**Solution 2 (Score: ~80-90):**
Your soil lacks nitrogen and has poor drainage. Stop daily watering immediately - clay soil needs water only when top 2 inches are dry. Add vermicompost (3-4 kg per plant) and mix sand or coco peat to improve drainage. Apply DAP fertilizer (50g per plant) around the base. Spray liquid seaweed extract every 10 days. Check for root rot by examining roots - if brown and mushy, reduce water more.
